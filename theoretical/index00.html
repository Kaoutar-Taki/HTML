Semantic Tags

Semantic HTML tags are tags that define the meaning of the content they contain. For example, the tags header, article and footer are semantic HTML tags. They clearly indicate the role of the content they contain.

Semantic HTML tags are important for a number of reasons:
  • They make your code more readable and maintainable.
  • They help search engines to better understand the content of your pages.
  • They make your pages more accessible to users with disabilities.

    • Here are some examples of semantic HTML tags:

    header: Defines the header of a web page, such as the logo and navigation menu.
    nav:Defines a navigation section, such as a sidebar or breadcrumb trail.
    section: Defines a section of a web page, such as a blog post or product listing.
    article: Defines a self-contained piece of content, such as a news article or blog post.
    footer: Defines the footer of a web page, such as copyright information and contact links.
    aside/details/summary: Defines content aside from the content (such as a sidebar) in the main part of a document.
